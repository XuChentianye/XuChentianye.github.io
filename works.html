[National Project] DefecTracker: An Industrial Screen Defect Detection System Based on Computer Vision and Automatic Optical Detection


College Students’ Innovative Entrepreneurial Training Plan Program (National Project)


Project Leader

Advisor: Prof. Zhe-Nan He

Oct 2021 - Sep 2022


I have developed an industrial screen defect detection system called DefecTracker, which solves the problems of low efficiency and poor accuracy of artificial defect detection in the industrial screen defect detection process. I totally developed three function modules for this system: static defect detection, dynamic defect detection, and industrial image denoising. Static defect detection is achieved by the improved Faster R-CNN model; dynamic defect detection is achieved by the Siam RPN++ model; industrial image denoising is achieved by the Cycle GAN model. What’s more, a complete Web system is implemented (click here to view), which provides a one-stop assistant platform for defect detection of industrial screens.

WisDomain: A Deep Learning Framework


Personal Work


Independent Developer

Aug 2021 - Dec 2021


Nowadays, neural network designers usually directly employ mature deep learning frameworks such as PyTorch or TensorFlow to build, train and use networks. However, because these frameworks are too highly encapsulated for users, it is difficult for beginners to understand the details of specific algorithms and mathematical principles if they only rely on these frameworks. Considering this, I only used python's basic libraries like NumPy to implement a lightweight and easy-to-expand deep learning framework WisDomain. All its modules are accompanied by detailed explanations, and users can add their own operators or other function expansions to it through simple codes, making it easier for beginners to learn and test the principles of neural networks. Whether it is a simple fully-connected neural network or a complex convolutional neural network, this framework can easily build and train it.

As Head of the Algorithm Department of Machine Learning and Data Science Association of Sichuan University, I used WisDomain to explain neural network algorithms and mathematical principles to association members, and received excellent teaching feedbacks. In addition, based on this project, I have successfully obtained a software patent (click here to view).



[National Project] Transformer-Based Pre-Trained Model for Medical Image


College Students’ Innovative Entrepreneurial Training Plan Program (National Project)


Core Project Member

Advisor: Prof. Jian-Cheng Lyu

Oct 2021 - Sep 2022


In actual cases of neural network applications, medical image data is often small in quantity and low in quality, which brings a big challenge to the training of network models. However, medical images have some common features compared with general images. For example, most medical images are black and white and have similar patterns. Based on the Transformer structure, we built a pre-trained model to learn the common features of medical images, which improved the performance of neural networks on medical images and shortened the training time. At the same time, we explored innovative applications of Transformer, developed a Web platform, and deployed deep learning models, providing convenient, fast, and accurate diagnostic methods for doctors.

In this project, I was mainly responsible for building and training the Transformer networks, testing and evaluating the models to obtain those with the best generalization performance on a variety of medical images.



[National Project] Pulmonary Nodule Follow-Up Management System Based on Artificial Intelligence


College Students’ Innovative Entrepreneurial Training Plan Program (National Project)


Core Project Member

Advisor: Prof. Ji-Xiang Guo

Jan 2021 - Oct 2021


Lung cancer develops from early pulmonary nodules, which is a serious threat to patients’ lives. It is very important to prevent lung cancer through early diagnosis and early treatment of pulmonary nodules. However, there are some problems in actual diagnoses, such as inaccurate manual film-reading and visual fatigue caused by mass reading. The project members developed a pulmonary nodule follow-up management system based on AI, which employs the Faster R-CNN model to detect pulmonary nodules, the GAN model to improve the image registration algorithm, and the Lung-RADS to determine the rate of nodal deterioration, and finally generates intelligent diagnostic reports, providing clinicians with a one-stop intelligent pulmonary nodule follow-up assistant platform.

In this project, I was responsible for the design of network architecture and the building of Web system, including the building, training, and optimization of the Faster R-CNN model, the design of front-end modules, as well as the interaction between the front-end and the back-end.

[College-Level Project] A New Calibration Strategy for Small Depth-of-Field Cameras Based on Deep Learning


Spark Program of Wu Yuzhang Honors College


Core Project Member

Advisor: Prof. Wan-Zhong Song

Jan 2021 - Oct 2021


One of the basic tasks of computer vision is to calculate the geometric information of objects in 3-D space from the 2-D image information obtained by cameras, and then rebuild and identify the objects. The relationship between the 3-D geometric position of a point on the surface of a 3-D object and its corresponding point in the image is determined by camera parameters. Under most conditions, these parameters must be obtained experimentally. The calibration of cameras is a very critical step in both image measurement and computer vision applications. However, in practice, existing calibration methods cannot be used to calibrate cameras with a small depth of field. For example, in the chessboard calibration method, the size of the calibration board is limited by the depth of field, so the rotation angle of the calibration board cannot be changed significantly, making the model obtained not accurate enough to be used in practice. Considering this, we innovatively used neural network models to learn camera parameters, which effectively solved the calibration problem of small depth-of-field cameras.

In this project, I was responsible for building, training, and improving neural network models, and testing the networks on oral cavity endoscopes.
